Doing this assignment, the engines acted all very similar. They all have a docking panel layout, with scene view, assets, heirarchy, and details panel. Importing was a simple drag and drop. For Godot and Unity I had to manually add the texture, but I didn't need to remake any materials or anything. However, for godot I did have to reimport all the assets, but that was just one button click.


In terms of building the scene, they were also very similar. I could simply drag and drop the assets into the scene view and then use transform, rotate, and scale widgets to move the objects. All 3 engines have a hierarchical scheme, so you can simply drag objects onto each other in the hierarchy to create parents. In Godot, there is no difference between components and gameobjects/actors, but for this use case there was not really a difference.
